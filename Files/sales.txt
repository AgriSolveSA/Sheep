You need a sales funnel that turns a skeptical, time‑poor, tech‑limited farmer into a paying customer who uses your platform repeatedly. Below is a funnel built for the South African sheep farming context – low‑tech, high‑trust, and action‑driven.

---

🧠 Core Insight

Farmers don’t buy software. They buy more profit and less headache. Your funnel must prove value before they pay, and make payment as frictionless as a bag of feed.

📣 Stage 1: Awareness (Cost: Almost Zero)

Tactic How to Execute Cost
WhatsApp broadcast Join local farming WhatsApp groups. Share a 30‑second voice note: “I help farmers find R5k+ waste. Send ‘INFO’ for free sample.” Free
Co‑op notice board Print A4 flyers with tear‑off phone number. Offer: “Free sample – SMS FARM to 082 xxx.” ~R50 for 20 flyers
Facebook groups Post in “South African Sheep Farmers” group. Share a screenshot of a sample action sheet (blurred, but shows checkboxes). Free
Radio ad (local station) 15‑second spot during agricultural hour. Cost R200‑R500 per play in small towns. R500‑R2,000
Spaza shop poster Put up a small poster: “Scan QR code, get your farm profit plan.” Spaza owner gets R20 per sign‑up. R0 (rev share)

Call to Action: “Download free sample report at shepherdai.co.za/sample” (no sign‑in required).

---

🤝 Stage 2: Trust & Engagement (The “Try Before You Buy”)

Farmers need to see their own potential savings without giving away the full report.

Tool: Free Savings Estimator (one‑page HTML on your site)

· Farmer enters: flock size, land size, breed.
· Your model calculates a rough potential saving (e.g., “Based on your inputs, we estimate you could save R8,400/year.”)
· No report, no PDF – just a number.
· Then ask: “Want the full plan? Pay R499.”

Why this works: It’s zero risk, takes 30 seconds, and shows immediate relevance.

Additional Trust Builders:

· A 2‑min video testimonial (you can film a neighbour farmer).
· A WhatsApp number that auto‑replies with FAQ (e.g., “How do I pay without a card?”).
· A “Farmer‑to‑Farmer” referral discount.

---

💰 Stage 3: Conversion (One‑Time Plan)

After the farmer sees their savings estimate, present:

“Your personalised one‑page action sheet is ready. It tells you exactly what to do this week, this month, this season. Includes supplier phone numbers and expected savings. Pay R499 once – or R99/month for quarterly updates.”

Payment options (in order of preference):

Method Steps
PayFast (online) Standard checkout. Works for 60% of farmers.
MoMo Pay Provide merchant code. Farmer pays via their mobile, you get instant notification.
Voucher (cash) Farmer buys a voucher at a local spaza (you set up via Kazang). Enters code on your site.
Manual bank deposit Provide FNB account number. Farmer sends proof of payment. You manually generate report (within 24h).

Upsell at payment success:

“Add monthly membership for R99 – first month free, cancel anytime. You get quarterly benchmark updates and access to the private farmer group.”

---

🔁 Stage 4: Ecosystem Retention (Recurring Revenue)

Once a farmer is a member (or even just a one‑time buyer), keep them engaged:

Activity Frequency How
Quarterly email Every 3 months “Your updated action sheet is ready – login to download.”
WhatsApp community Ongoing Members get a private group. Share tips, market prices, weather alerts.
Referral program Every referral “Bring another farmer – you get R100 off your next purchase or membership fee.”
Seasonal reminders Monthly “Plant lucerne now.” “Shear next week.”
Add‑on module teasers Monthly “Track your stock with our new module – members get 20% off.”

Goal: Turn one‑time buyers into members (R99/m) and keep them for at least 6‑12 months.

💬 Final Word

Your funnel is not a fancy marketing machine. It’s a conversation that starts with a free sample, proves value with a personalised savings number, and ends with a simple, cash‑friendly payment. Farmers don’t need a 10‑step automation – they need a neighbour who says, “Try this, it worked for me.”

Go start that conversation.
