🧾 Guide Structure (Template)

Every guide follows the same 6‑page layout (printable PDF):

Page Content
1 Title + Expected time + Difficulty (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced)
2 Tools & materials (checklist)
3 Step‑by‑step (numbered, simple language, photos/diagrams where possible)
4 Common mistakes + Troubleshooting
5 Safety & disclaimers (mandatory)
6 Next steps (link to related guides, ecosystem membership, vet referral)

📦 How to Create Guides (Low‑Cost, High‑Quality)

Option A: Write them yourself (cheapest, but time‑consuming)

· Use your own knowledge + cross‑check with standard texts (e.g., Sheep Farming for South Africa by D. le Roux).
· Hire a freelance agricultural writer (Upwork / Fiverr) – R200‑500 per guide.

Option B: License existing content (fast, medium cost)

· Approach agricultural colleges (Elsenburg, Cedara, Grootfontein) – they often have extension guides they allow republishing with credit.
· Join SA Stud Book or NWGA – members may share content.

Option C: AI‑assisted + vet review (fast, scalable)

· Use GPT‑4 to generate a first draft from a detailed prompt.
· Have a practising vet (hourly rate R500‑1,000) review medical guides – charge a premium for “vet‑approved” badge.

Recommended mix: Write 10 core guides yourself (or with AI), then commission vets for 5 high‑value medical guides (priced at R149‑199).
