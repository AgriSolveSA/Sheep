🐄 Module 2: Cattle (Beef & Dairy)

Sub‑module Unique Data Points Source of Benchmarks
Beef Carrying capacity (ha/cow), weaning rate (%), feedlot conversion, beef prices (R/kg), hide value Red Meat Levy SA, SAMIC, feedlot studies
Dairy Litres/cow/day, milk price (R/L), calving interval, AI costs, veterinary protocols Milk SA, Lactodata

Timeline to build: 3‑4 weeks (once sheep module is stable).

Key inefficiencies (high SI) for cattle:

· Feedlot rations – similar to sheep (SI ≈ 2‑4)
· Veterinary (especially dairy) – antibiotics, supplements (SI ≈ 8‑10)
· Water infrastructure – even larger demand (SI ≈ 10)
· Breeding (bulls) – similar SI to stud rams (8‑12)

Action sheet will include: rotational grazing plans, AI vs bull economics, milk cooling efficiency.

🐐 Module 3: Goats

Sub‑module Unique Data Benchmarks

Angora (mohair) Mohair yield (kg/goat), price (R/kg), shearing twice/year Cape Mohair, SAMIC

🐖 Module 4: Pigs

Data Points Farrowing rate, weaning to finish conversion, feed cost (pig meal), pork prices (R/kg)
Sources SAPPO (South African Pork Producers’ Organisation)

Timeline: 2 weeks.

Key inefficiencies: feed conversion (largest cost), housing ventilation (electricity), veterinary (SI high for vaccines).

---

🐔 Module 5: Poultry (Broilers & Layers)

Sub‑module Data Sources
Broilers Day‑old chick price, feed conversion (2:1), slaughter age (42 days), breast meat price SAPA (SA Poultry Association)
Layers Eggs/hen/day, feed cost per dozen, culling value EggSA

Timeline: 2 weeks.

Inefficiencies: feed (60‑70% of cost), mortality (high in broilers), egg grading and packaging markups.

---

🌾 Module 6: Mixed Farming (Sheep + Crops)

This module integrates sheep and cropping. The engine calculates:

· Crop residues (maize stalks, sorghum) as winter feed → reduces purchased feed cost.
· Manure from sheep as fertiliser for crops → reduces fertiliser cost.
· Rotational grazing on fallow land → improves soil health.

Timeline: 4 weeks (requires crop yield tables, fertiliser prices, soil type adjustments).

Inefficiencies: over‑purchased fertiliser, under‑utilised residues, transport of manure.

---

🌽 Module 7: Pure Cropping (Arable)

Crop Types Maize, wheat, soybeans, sunflowers, lucerne (forage)
Data Yield per ha, input costs (seed, fertiliser, chemicals), market price per ton, storage costs
Sources Grain SA, SAGIS, AMT

Timeline: 4‑6 weeks.

Inefficiencies (SI):

· Fertiliser (raw materials R5/kg → delivered R15/kg → SI 3)
· Seed (genetically modified premiums)
· Chemicals (similar to vet meds – SI 8‑10)
· Transport to silo
