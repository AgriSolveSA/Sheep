📋 What KYC Information to Collect

Field Required For How to Verify
Full name & surname All users Match with ID document
South African ID number All users Validate checksum (Luhn‑like algorithm for SA ID)
Mobile number All users SMS OTP (one‑time PIN)
Email address All users Click verification link
Physical address Sellers (to post ads) Proof of address (municipal bill, bank statement, lease agreement) – upload PDF/photo
Bank account details Sellers (to receive payments) Bank confirmation letter or small test deposit (R0.01)
ID document copy Sellers Upload photo of ID front/back; optional automated check via ScreenlyyID (free tier)

You do not need to collect all of this upfront. A progressive KYC approach is less intimidating:

1. Sign‑up: Name + email + mobile (SMS OTP).
2. To browse & inquire: No further docs.
3. To post first ad: Ask for ID number + upload ID photo + proof of address.
4. To receive payout: Ask for bank account details.

This way, a user who only wants to browse or ask a question never hits the heavy KYC wall.

🛡️ How to Store KYC Data (Securely & Compliantly)

Requirement Implementation
Data encryption at rest Use AES‑256 for stored documents (e.g., crypto in Node.js, or store files outside web root with random names)
Data encryption in transit HTTPS only (Let's Encrypt)
Access control Only admin users (you) can view full KYC documents; regular users see only their own
Retention policy Keep for 5 years after account closure (per FICA) then permanently delete
Privacy policy Must state: “We collect ID, address, bank details for fraud prevention and to comply with South African law. We do not sell your data.”
Data breach notification Have a procedure to notify affected users and the Information Regulator within 72 hours (POPIA requirement)

You can use SQLite to store metadata (verified flags, document hashes), but store the actual uploaded files on disk (not in the database). Example folder structure:

```
/data/kyc/
   user_123/
      id_front.jpg
      id_back.jpg
      proof_of_address.pdf
   user_456/
      ...
```

```

⬜ Upload ID document
⬜ Upload proof of address
⬜ Link bank account

[Start verification] button
```

Step 3: Verification form (one page)

· Upload ID (front + back) – drag & drop or camera capture.
· Upload proof of address (PDF or photo) – utility bill, bank statement, lease (dated within last 3 months).
· Enter bank account details (account holder, bank, branch code, account number).

Submit → Status changes to “KYC under review (typically 24 hours)”.

Step 4: Admin review (you)

· Log into admin panel, see pending verifications.
· Check that uploaded documents are legible and match the user’s name/address.
· Approve or reject with reason (e.g., “Proof of address is older than 3 months”).

Step 5: Approval notification

· Email + dashboard alert: “Your account is now verified. You can post ads.”
· User can now access ad posting form.

⚖️ Legal & Compliance Notes

Law Relevance
FICA (Financial Intelligence Centre Act) If you facilitate payments between buyers and sellers (e.g., escrow or direct payouts), you must register as an accountable institution with the FIC. KYC is mandatory.
POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act) You must have a privacy policy, get consent to collect KYC data, and allow users to request deletion of their data (subject to legal retention).
Consumer Protection Act If a user is defrauded on your platform despite KYC, you may have residual liability. Add terms of use that limit your liability and require users to report disputes.

Recommendation: Spend R5,000–10,000 on a one‑time legal review of your KYC and terms of use. It will save you much more in potential fines or lawsuits.
